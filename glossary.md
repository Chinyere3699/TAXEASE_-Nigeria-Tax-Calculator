# Glossary 

## 1. PAYE (Pay As You Earn): The tax deducted directly from your salary by your employer and paid to the government.

## 2. Taxable Income: The part of your income that actually gets taxed.

## 3. Relief: A legal deduction that lowers the income you pay tax on.

## 4. Pension: Retirement savings deducted before tax payment (lowers your tax bill).

## 5. NHF (National Housing Fund): Housing fund contribution (also lowers your tax). 

## 6. Gross Income: Total earnings before deductions.

## 7. Taxable Income: The portion of your income left after removing reliefs and contributions. This is what tax is actually calculated on.

## 8. Net Salary (Take-Home Pay): The amount you actually receive after all deductions 

## 9. CRA (Consolidated Relief Allowance) : A legal tax reduction. It lowers your taxable income automatically, you don’t pay tax on this portion.

## 10. Payroll:  The process of calculating employee salaries, taxes, pensions, deductions, and payments.

## 11. Minimum Tax: A small tax amount you pay if your calculated tax is very low or zero to ensure everyone contributes something.

## 12. Monthly Tax: Your tax calculated per month. 

## 13. Annual Tax: Your tax calculated for a full year.

## 14. Tax Summary / Report: A downloadable document showing how your tax was calculated. This is helpful for records or loan applications.

## 15. Tax Bands: The different tax rates for different income ranges.
