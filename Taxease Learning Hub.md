# General Tax Questions
 
## 1. What are the Nigerian tax rates for 2026?
​Under the Nigeria Tax Act 2025, the 2026 personal income tax rates follow a progressive system:

0% on the first          ₦   800,000

​15% on ₦800,001     -    ₦ 3,000,000

​18% on ₦3,000,001   -    ₦12,000,000

​21% on ₦12,000,001  -    ₦25,000,000

​23% on ₦25,000,001  -    ₦50,000,000

​25% on income above      ₦50,000,000

​This progressive structure ensures that your effective tax rate is always lower than your top marginal tax bracket.

## 2. How much can I earn tax-free in Nigeria?
​The tax-free threshold for 2026 is ₦800,000 per year (approximately ₦66,667 per month).

This represents a significant increase from the historic ~₦300,000 threshold. 

If your annual earnings fall below ₦800,000, you owe zero tax, though tracking your income remains vital for legal documentation.

## ​3. When is the tax filing deadline in Nigeria?
​The annual tax filing deadline for individuals is **March 31** of each year for the previous year's income. 

For 2026 earnings, you must file your return by March 31, 2027. 

Other crucial deadlines include monthly VAT returns (due by the 21st of the following month) and monthly PAYE remittances (due by the 10th of the following month).

Late filing attracts steep administrative penalties

# Tax Rates & Brackets Questions

## 1. How do progressive tax brackets work?
​Nigeria utilizes a progressive tax system where successive portions of your income are taxed at increasingly higher rates. 

For example, if you earn ₦5,000,000:

​The first ₦800,000 is taxed at 0%

​The next ₦2,200,000 (the band from ₦800,001 to ₦3,000,000) is taxed at 15%

​The remaining ₦2,000,000 is taxed at 18%

​Because the higher rates only apply to income within those specific bands, your actual effective tax rate comes out to approximately 13.8%, rather than a flat 18%.

## ​2. What is the VAT rate in Nigeria?
​Under the current legislative framework, the standard Value Added Tax (VAT) rate in Nigeria is 10%. 

VAT applies to most commercial supplies of goods and services. However, essential items such as basic food products, medical services, educational materials, and baby products are legally exempt. 

Furthermore, all residential property sales, leases, and rent are completely VAT-exempt to support affordable housing. 

Businesses with an annual turnover of ₦50 million or more are legally required to register for VAT, charge it on their invoices, and remit it monthly.

## ​3. Are company tax rates different from personal income tax?
​Yes. Under the current tax regime, corporate entities are taxed based on their annual turnover bands:

​Small Companies (Turnover ≤ ₦50 Million): 0% Company Income Tax (CIT)

​Medium Companies (Turnover ₦50 Million – ₦500 Million): 20% CIT

​Large Companies (Turnover > ₦500 Million): 25% CIT

​Additionally, qualifying companies are subject to a 4% Consolidated Development Levy on their assessable profits. 

This levy was introduced to simplify corporate taxation by consolidating the old Tertiary Education Tax (TET) and other fragmented statutory levies into a single, streamlined compliance mechanism.

# Freelancer Tax Questions

## ​1. Do freelancers pay tax in Nigeria?

​Yes, freelancers, digital creators, and independent contractors are legally required to pay personal income tax on all global earnings above the annual ₦800,000 threshold. 

As self-employed individuals, freelancers operate under a self-assessment tax model, meaning you are personally responsible for calculating, filing, and paying your taxes.

Freelancers are subject to the exact same progressive personal income tax brackets (15% to 25%) as standard corporate employees. 

Annual returns must be filed by March 31 for the preceding year's income.
​
## 2. Is foreign income taxable in Nigeria?
​Yes, individuals classified as Nigerian tax residents are subject to tax on their worldwide income, which legally includes all earnings derived from foreign clients. 

Any foreign currency income earned (such as USD, GBP, or EUR) must be converted into Naira using the official CBN operational conversion rates applicable at the exact time the funds were received. 

This rule strictly applies to all remote work, international contracts, and digital freelance platforms like Upwork and Fiverr.

## ​3. What expenses can freelancers deduct from taxes?
​Freelancers can significantly lower their taxable income by deducting legitimate business expenses. 

Allowable deductions include work equipment (laptops, smartphones, cameras, microphones), professional software subscriptions, internet data plans, home office utility allocations, professional training courses, advertising costs, bank transaction charges, professional agency fees (lawyers, accountants), workspace travel, and co-working space membership fees. 

You must retain clear digital receipts for all claimed deductions as strict evidence for audit purposes.
